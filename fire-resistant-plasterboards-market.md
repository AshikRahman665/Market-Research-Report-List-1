<p><p>The fire-resistant plasterboards market is characterized by two main types: tapered-edged and square-edged boards. Tapered-edged plasterboards are designed for seamless jointing, allowing for smooth finishes and minimizing gaps, making them ideal for ceilings and walls where aesthetics and performance are critical. In contrast, square-edged plasterboards offer a straightforward installation for basic applications, providing solid fire resistance without the need for complex finishing. Both types play important roles in enhancing fire safety in residential and commercial constructions.</p></p>
