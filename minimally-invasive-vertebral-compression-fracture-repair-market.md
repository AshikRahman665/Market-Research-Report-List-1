<p><p>The Minimally Invasive Vertebral Compression Fracture Repair market primarily includes two types of devices: vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices. Vertebroplasty involves the injection of bone cement directly into the fractured vertebra to stabilize it, while kyphoplasty includes an additional step of inflating a balloon to create a space before cement injection, aiming to restore vertebral height and shape. Both procedures aim to alleviate pain and improve mobility for patients suffering from vertebral compression fractures, contributing to their growing market demand.</p></p>
