<p><p>The Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) treatments market comprises three main types: fluid replacement, electrolyte replacement, and insulin therapy. Fluid replacement is critical to rehydrate patients and restore blood volume, often using saline solutions. Electrolyte replacement addresses imbalances, particularly with potassium, to prevent complications. Insulin therapy is the cornerstone of DKA treatment, as it helps lower blood glucose levels and halts ketone production. Together, these therapies are essential for stabilizing patients and managing the acute effects of DKA.</p></p>
