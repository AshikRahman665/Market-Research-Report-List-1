<p><p>The Sub 24 GHz ISM radio transceiver market encompasses various modulation types, each suited for specific applications. FSK (Frequency Shift Keying) and GFSK (Gaussian FSK) are used for efficient data transmission. MSK (Minimum Shift Keying) offers improved spectral efficiency. OOK (On-Off Keying) and ASK (Amplitude Shift Keying) are simpler forms suited for low-complexity applications. GMSK (Gaussian MSK) combines GFSK benefits with better performance. FM (Frequency Modulation) is used for analog signals, while LoRa (Long Range) enables low-power, long-distance communication, and PSK (Phase Shift Keying) supports robust data transmission in noisy environments.</p></p>
