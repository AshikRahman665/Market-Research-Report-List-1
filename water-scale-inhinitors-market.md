<p><p>Water scale inhibitors are chemicals designed to prevent the formation of scale in water systems. The market is divided into two main types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic scale inhibitors typically include phosphonates and polyphosphates, which work through precipitation or complexation to reduce scale formation. Organic scale inhibitors, on the other hand, are often polymers or natural compounds that prevent scaling through dispersal and threshold effects. Both types are essential in various industries, including oil and gas, water treatment, and cooling systems.</p></p>
