<p><p>The silicon interposers market is categorized into 2D, 2.5D, and 3D segments, each defined by their packaging and integration capabilities. </p><p>2D interposers involve traditional flat layouts for chip designs. </p><p>2.5D interposers utilize a silicon interposer layer to connect multiple chips vertically, enabling higher bandwidth and reduced latency. </p><p>3D interposers stack multiple chips directly, facilitating even greater integration and performance while minimizing space. These distinctions impact applications in high-performance computing, AI, and advanced telecommunications.</p></p>
